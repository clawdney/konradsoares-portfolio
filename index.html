01About Me

"It started with curiosity at age 12..."

Before the internet existed in Brazil, I discovered my passion for technology. There were no online tutorials, no Google - just a burning curiosity and a willingness to learn.

I started with basic courses on computers that weren't even XT286s yet. Then came my first own machine - an MSX - loading programs from 5¼" floppies and cassettes. Those days taught me patience and the thrill of making things work.

Technical school in data processing changed everything. I got my hands on an XT286, then a 386 with Soundblaster multimedia and a US Robotics modem. Hardware troubleshooting, building systems, learning Clipper, COBOL, dBase - this is where the foundation was laid.

While studying network topology, I discovered the company's network was still in star topology using old cabling - disconnecting one computer would bring down the entire network. But the real breakthrough was finding a flaw in their Clipper system: pressing enter repeatedly during database append operations and hitting pause would crash the entire company-wide system. I reported it, got promoted, and learned my first lesson in cybersecurity.

Those late-night hours had another benefit too - I figured out how to hack local ISPs to get free internet access after midnight when phone calls were cheaper. Downloading MP3s on Napster became my reward for studying hard.

From there, I moved into Delphi and ColdFusion development, then Windows infrastructure. An MCSA certification made me the go-to person for Microsoft troubleshooting. The journey continued into Linux and middleware.

Today, as Principal Cloud Specialist at OpenText, I blend technical SaaS support, DevOps, and cloud engineering - driven by a love for complex troubleshooting, helping customers succeed, and turning chaos into reliable systems.

20+ years later, the curiosity still burns.
